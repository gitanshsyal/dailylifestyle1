From Wheelchair to the Captionship of Indian Hockey team-An Inspiring tale of Sandeep Singh Bhinder
Sandeep Singh Bhinder,a name that every hockey lover is found of.There are indeed several reasons to love this great Indian hockey player,but most notable part is Sandeep Singh Bhinder's inspiring life story.
Sandeep made his international debut in january 2004 in Sultan Azlan Shah Cup in Kuala Lumpur.During the Peak of his wonderful career,Sandeep was seriously injured after being hit by an accidental gunshot in the shatabadi train on 22 august 2006,While on his way to join the national team which was to leave for the World Cup in Germany two days later.At time of incident he felt like "as if a hot iron rod was inserted into his back".With blackness surrounding his eyes,he heard a voice saying 'merese goli chal gayi hai'.After a while,his body gradually became numb and his legs were unable to move.The train was stopped at Kurukshetra railway station and he was carried to hospital on a stretcher which was used to remove dead bodies from railway track.At that time he was saying to himself he don't want to die.His entire Body became paraylsed and he was told by doctor that he would never be able to stand again and also never be able to return on hockey ground again.But He overcomed this hardest phase of his life.Due to his efforts and also of his family,his elder brother Bikram and Indian hockey federation he was able to stand on his feet again and able to return back to hockey field again.In 2009,He beame the Caption of The Indian national team.Under his Captaincy,The Indian team won the Sultan Azad shah cup in 2009 after 13 years and he was the top scorer of this tournament.At that time,he made a world record of world 's best spped in drag flick of speed 145 km/hr.
In 2012,Indian men's hockey team qualified for summer olympics in London after a gap of 8 years.World class drag-flicker Sandeep Singh scored 5 goals in the final against France including a hat-trick-all from penalty corners and he was highest scorer of the tournament.
